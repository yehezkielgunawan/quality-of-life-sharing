# The "Cognitive Offloading" Trap

<div class="text-lg">

**Cognitive offloading** = letting an external system handle what your brain used to do.

</div>

<v-click>

<div class="mt-6 bg-yellow-50 dark:bg-yellow-900/20 p-4 rounded-lg">

### What it looks like in real life:

- You used to <b>brainstorm</b> ideas → Now you ask AI for "10 essay topics"
- You used to <b>struggle</b> through a proof → Now you paste the question and wait
- You used to <b>compare</b> sources and form an opinion → Now you accept the first coherent paragraph

</div>

</v-click>

<v-click>

<p class="mt-6 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: Gerlich (2025), "AI Tools in Society: Impacts on Cognitive Offloading" — Societies 15(1), 6.
</p>

</v-click>

<!--
Cognitive offloading isn't always bad. We offload math to calculators, navigation to GPS. But when we offload reasoning itself, we start to lose the muscle.
-->

# The Data: Inference & Evaluation Are Dropping

::left::

<div class="text-lg">

A study of **200 first-year students in Peru** (Vereau Amaya et al., 2025) found:

- **Negative correlation (r = -.36)** between AI dependence and overall critical thinking
- **Inference skills** most affected (r = -.41)
- **Evaluation skills** also vulnerable (r = -.33)

</div>

::right::

<v-click>

<div class="bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 p-4 rounded-lg text-center">

<div class="text-4xl font-bold text-red-500">r = -.41</div>
<div class="mt-2">AI Dependence ↔ Inference Ability</div>
<div class="text-sm opacity-60 mt-2">The stronger the AI habit, the weaker the "what can I conclude from this?" skill.</div>

</div>

</v-click>

<v-click>

<p class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: Vereau Amaya et al. (2025), "Between Assistance and Dependence," IJLTER 24(12), 6.
</p>

</v-click>

<!--
Inference is the skill of drawing logical conclusions from evidence. It's basically what every college assignment asks you to do. And it's the exact thing most damaged by AI dependency.
-->

# The Experiment: High AI Access vs. Restricted Use

<div class="text-lg">

A **quasi-experimental study** (Qurat ul Ain & Rizwan, 2026) split students into two groups:

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-6">

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg bg-green-50 dark:bg-green-900/10">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-600">Control Group</div>
  <div class="mt-2">Limited AI use, traditional methods</div>
  <div class="mt-4 font-bold">Result: No significant change in critical thinking</div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/10">
  <div class="font-bold text-red-600">Experimental Group</div>
  <div class="mt-2">High independent AI use allowed</div>
  <div class="mt-4 font-bold">Result: Significant decline in critical thinking</div>
</div>

</div>

<p class="mt-6 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: Qurat ul Ain & Rizwan (2026), "Does Over-Reliance on AI Cause Reduction in Critical Thinking?" JALT.
</p>

<!--
This is one of the clearest causal pieces of evidence we have. Same students, same baseline — but the ones who went all-in on AI saw their critical thinking drop. The control group stayed stable.
-->

# The Paradox: AI Doesn't Kill Thinking — Passivity Does

<div class="text-lg">

Not all AI use is bad. The difference is <b>how</b> you use it:

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-6">

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-red-500">❌ Passive Use (The Substitute)</div>
  <div class="mt-2 text-sm">
    - Copy-paste the output<br/>
    - Accept the first answer<br/>
    - Skip the struggle phase<br/>
    <b>Result:</b> Critical thinking atrophies
  </div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-500">✅ Reflective Use (The Scaffold)</div>
  <div class="mt-2 text-sm">
    - Debate the AI's answer<br/>
    - Fact-check with sources<br/>
    - Use it to expand, not replace<br/>
    <b>Result:</b> Critical thinking can grow
  </div>
</div>

</div>

<p class="mt-6 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: MDPI Education Sciences (2025), "AI-Generated Content Empowers College Students' Critical Thinking Skills" — the key is reflection. Hou et al. (2025), BJET.
</p>

<!--
This is actually hopeful. It means the tool isn't the villain — the habit is. We can change the habit. But it takes intention.
-->

# The Loneliness Paradox

<div class="text-lg">

You'd think talking to an always-available AI would make you <b>less</b> lonely.

</div>

<v-click>

<div class="mt-6 bg-blue-50 dark:bg-blue-900/20 p-4 rounded-lg">

### What research actually shows:

Students who seek <b>social support from AI</b> report <b>higher loneliness</b> than those who seek support from friends, family, or even professors.

AI acts as a <b>palliative</b> — it soothes the symptom without treating the cause.

</div>

</v-click>

<v-click>

<p class="mt-4 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: Crawford (2024), "When AI Substitutes Humans in Higher Education," Studies in Higher Education. <br/>
  Also: Xie, Pentina & Hancock (2023).
</p>

</v-click>

<!--
This one hits hard. I know people who'd rather message ChatGPT at 2am than text a friend. The AI never judges, never sleeps, never says "I'm busy." But that comfort comes at a cost.
-->

# The Hidden Costs: Memory & Procrastination

<div class="text-lg">

Abbas et al. (2024) tracked **494 students** across three waves:
</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-6">

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-xl mb-2">🧠 Memory Loss</div>
  <div class="text-sm">
    Heavy ChatGPT users reported <b>difficulty recalling information</b> they "learned" through AI. Why? Because active cognitive struggle is what <b>cements</b> memory.
  </div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-xl mb-2">⏰ Procrastination</div>
  <div class="text-sm">
    Students with high AI use showed <b>higher procrastination</b>. The logic: "I can get this done in 30 minutes with AI, so I'll start in 30 minutes."
  </div>
</div>

</div>

<p class="mt-6 text-sm opacity-60">
  Source: Abbas et al. (2024), "Is It Harmful or Helpful?" Int. J. Educ. Technol. High. Educ. 21, 10.
</p>

<!--
Memory loss and procrastination are the quiet killers of college success. You don't notice them until exam week hits and your brain feels like a sieve.
-->

# Pre-AI Habits vs. Post-AI Habits

::left::

<div class="text-sm">

### 📚 The Old Way (Pre-AI)

- Go to the library, browse physical shelves
- Stare at a blank doc for an hour
- Email the TA and wait 2 days
- Form study groups by actually meeting
- Fail, retry, learn the hard way
- Write a terrible first draft, then improve it

</div>

::right::

<div class="text-sm">

### 🤖 The New Way (Post-AI)

- Prompt engineer from bed at 2am
- Generate 3 drafts in 10 minutes, pick one
- Ask ChatGPT to explain instead of the prof
- "Study group" = shared prompts in Discord
- Rarely fail because AI catches you
- Polish an AI draft, call it "editing"

</div>

<v-click>

<div class="mt-4 bg-red-50 dark:bg-red-900/20 p-3 rounded text-sm">
  <b>Question:</b> Which version of you is going to handle a job interview, a real crisis, or a relationship problem better?
</div>

</v-click>

<!--
I'm not romanticizing the old way. It was slower and sometimes frustrating. But it built something inside us that the new way skips.
-->

</div>

<v-click>

<div class="mt-6 text-center">

# Practical Habits for Healthier AI Use

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-4">

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-600">1. The Blank Page Rule</div>
  <div class="text-sm mt-2">
    Spend <b>15 minutes</b> thinking or writing before opening any AI tool. Force your brain to warm up first.
  </div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-600">2. The Devil's Advocate</div>
  <div class="text-sm mt-2">
    For every AI answer, ask: <b>"What's wrong with this?"</b> Then go find a source that disagrees.
  </div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-600">3. The Human Checkpoint</div>
  <div class="text-sm mt-2">
    Before asking ChatGPT, ask a classmate or TA. Even if it takes longer, the social connection matters.
  </div>
</div>

<div class="p-4 border rounded-lg">
  <div class="font-bold text-green-600">4. The Source Audit</div>
  <div class="text-sm mt-2">
    If you use AI for research, <b>verify at least one citation independently.</b> Hallucinations are real.
  </div>
</div>

</div>
